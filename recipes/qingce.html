Qince Stir Fry

Description

This is a great vegetarian recipe and will taste delicious (I never made it myself so try at your own risk)! This dish will be packed with asian flavors and a slight kick. You mouth will love the crunch from the lotus root and the chewiness of the mushrooms.

The star of this dish will be the lotus root and some of you may not be familiar with what a lotus root is, so here's a photo.

Ingredients

  • 4 oz Lotus root
  • 2 oz Shimeji mushrooms
  • 2 oz Dry Shitake mushrooms (soaked)
  • 2 red chili peppers (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 green onion chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon fish sauce
  • few leaves of lettuce (mostly for plating)

    • Steps

    • Prepare the Veggies

      1. Make sure to soak your dry shitake mushrooms in warm water for at least 30 minutes.
      2. Wash all the vegetables.
      3. Using a peeler, peel the skin off the lotus root.
      4. Seperate your shimejji mushrooms from the base.
      5. Chop the lotus root into 1 inch slices. (becareful because the lotus root can be slippery).
      6. Chop the red chili peppers and green onions into small pieces.

    • Cooking the ingredients

      1. Add oil to a pan and let it warm up.
      2. Throw in the lotus roots first since this will take the longest to cook through.
      3. When the lotus root becomes a bit aromatic add the mushrooms, peppers and green onion and cook it for 8 mins on medium high heat.
      4. Now add the sugar and fish sauce and get it a good scramble.
      5. Adjust any of the seasonings accordingly to taste.
      6. Lastly plate the leaves of lettuce on a plate then pour the stirfry onto the plate and enjoy!
